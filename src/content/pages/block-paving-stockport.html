Block Paving Patterns Available in Stockport

The pattern you choose affects both the appearance and the structural strength of your driveway. We'll recommend the best option for your property — here are the most popular choices across Stockport.

Herringbone

The strongest laying pattern — blocks interlock at 45° or 90° to create a surface that resists movement under vehicle load. Ideal for driveways across Stockport.

How We Work

Our Block Paving Installation Process in Stockport

A quality block paving driveway starts long before the first block is laid. Our five-stage process ensures your Stockport driveway is built to last for decades.

1

Free Survey & Quote

We visit your Stockport property, assess access and ground conditions, discuss your design preferences, and provide a detailed written quote — at no cost.

2

Excavation

The existing surface is broken out and removed. We excavate to the correct depth for your ground conditions — typically 200–250mm — and remove all spoil from site.

3

Sub-base & Edging

A compacted MOT Type 1 sub-base is laid and mechanically compacted. Solid edge restraints are installed to keep the blocks in place permanently.

4

Block Laying

Blocks are laid in your chosen pattern on a sharp sand bed, cut precisely at edges, and mechanically compacted. Kiln-dried sand is brushed into the joints to lock everything in place.

5

Clean & Handover

All waste is removed, surrounding areas are fully cleaned and reinstated, and we walk you through your new driveway and guarantee before we leave.

Block Paving Stockport — FAQs

How much does block paving cost in Stockport?

Block paving driveways in Stockport typically cost between £50 and £120 per square metre depending on the block type chosen, the laying pattern, size of the area, and any groundwork required. Concrete blocks start from around £50/m², while premium tumbled clay blocks cost £80–£120/m². A typical 40m² driveway would cost £2,000–£4,800. We provide free, itemised quotes for all properties across Stockport.

How long does block paving last in Stockport?

Professionally installed block paving typically lasts 20–25 years for concrete blocks and 25–40 years for clay blocks. The key to longevity is a correctly specified and compacted sub-base — skimping on this is the most common cause of early failure. We always build to the right specification for your ground conditions, and back our work with a 10-year workmanship guarantee.

Do I need planning permission for block paving in Stockport?

Standard (non-permeable) block paving may require you to direct surface water away from the road if the area exceeds 5m². In practice this usually means grading the surface towards a lawn or border, or installing a simple linear drain. Alternatively, permeable block paving eliminates this requirement entirely. We'll advise you on the right approach for your specific property and Stockport postcode during the free site survey.

How long does block paving installation take?

A standard block paving driveway in Stockport typically takes 2–4 days to install, depending on the size of the area and complexity of the design. A straightforward herringbone driveway of around 40m² would typically be complete in 2 days. Larger driveways with feature circles, decorative edging or complex patterns may take 3–5 days. We'll give you a clear programme with your written quote.

Can you repair my existing block paving in Stockport?

Yes — block paving repair is one of the great advantages of this surface type. Individual blocks can be lifted and replaced, sunken areas can be re-laid with improved drainage, and loose or missing jointing sand can be replaced. We carry out block paving repairs, repointing, and jet washing and resealing across all of Stockport. Contact us for an honest assessment of whether repair or replacement is the better option for you.

What is the best block paving pattern for a driveway in Stockport?

Herringbone is widely regarded as the strongest pattern for driveways because the interlocking 45° or 90° angle resists horizontal movement under vehicle load. It's also the most popular choice across Stockport for this reason. Stretcher bond and basketweave are attractive alternatives for lower-traffic areas. Feature circles in a contrasting block colour are a popular decorative addition for larger driveways in Bramhall, Cheadle Hulme and Marple.
