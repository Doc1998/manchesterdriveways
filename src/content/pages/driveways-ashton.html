Most Popular

Block Paving

The most popular driveway choice in Ashton-under-Lyne. Herringbone, basketweave or stretcher bond patterns in concrete or tumbled clay blocks. Individually repairable — one of block paving's greatest advantages. Lasts 20–40 years with a correctly built sub-base.

From £50–£120/m²
Lifespan 20–40 years
Install Time 2–5 days
Low Maintenance

Resin Bound

A smooth, seamless, fully permeable surface — meaning no planning permission required regardless of size. UV-stable resins available in 30+ aggregate colours. No loose stones, no weeding, just an annual jet wash. Can be laid over existing tarmac or concrete if the base is sound.

From £40–£80/m²
Lifespan 15–25 years
Install Time 1–3 days
Decorative

Pattern Imprinted Concrete

Decorative concrete stamped to mimic cobbles, slate, brick or natural stone. A premium-looking finish at a mid-range price. Sealed on completion and re-sealing every 3–5 years keeps the colour and protection. 20+ year lifespan when correctly installed and maintained.

From £50–£90/m²
Lifespan 20+ years
Install Time 2–3 days + cure

Questions we hear regularly from homeowners across Ashton-under-Lyne and Tameside.

How much does a new driveway cost in Ashton-under-Lyne?

Costs vary by surface and size. Tarmac from around £30–50/m², block paving from £50–120/m², resin bound from £40–80/m², and pattern imprinted concrete from £50–90/m². A typical 40m² driveway in Ashton-under-Lyne costs approximately £1,200–£4,800. We provide free, detailed written quotes — no hidden costs.

Do I need planning permission for a driveway in Ashton-under-Lyne?

Most driveways in Ashton-under-Lyne don't require planning permission, provided drainage is handled correctly. Resin bound and permeable block paving are fully SuDS-compliant and never need permission. For non-permeable surfaces over 5m², water must drain to a lawn or soakaway rather than the road. We advise on this at the free site survey.

What's the best driveway surface for Ashton-under-Lyne homes?

Block paving is the most popular choice — it suits the area's housing stock well and is uniquely repairable (individual blocks can be lifted and replaced). Tarmac is the most cost-effective option. Resin bound gives a modern, clean finish with no planning permission needed. We'll always recommend what genuinely suits your property at the free survey.

How long does driveway installation take in Ashton-under-Lyne?

Tarmac: 1–2 days. Resin bound: 1–3 days. Block paving: 2–5 days depending on size and pattern. Pattern imprinted concrete: 2–3 days plus curing. We agree a programme before starting and keep to it — we know how disruptive driveway work can be and we aim to minimise disruption.

Do you guarantee driveway work in Ashton-under-Lyne?

Yes — all our driveway installations in Ashton-under-Lyne and across Tameside carry a 10-year workmanship guarantee. We've been trading since 1994 and stand behind every job we do. If something fails due to workmanship within 10 years, we return and fix it at no charge.

Can you repair my existing driveway in Ashton-under-Lyne?

Yes. We carry out block paving repairs and relay, repointing, tarmac patching and full resurfacing across all of Ashton-under-Lyne and Tameside. At the free site survey we'll assess the existing surface honestly — if repair is the better value option we'll say so, even if it means a smaller job for us.
