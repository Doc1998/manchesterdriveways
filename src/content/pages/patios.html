Most Popular

Block Paving Patios

Concrete, tegula or tumbled clay blocks in a wide range of colours and patterns. Individually repairable — if a block cracks or a pipe needs access, individual units can be lifted and relaid without disturbing the rest of the patio. Good kerb appeal, long lifespan.

Lifespan20–40 years
Install2–4 days

Most Popular — Modern Finish

Porcelain Patios

Large-format porcelain slabs give a sleek, contemporary finish that's virtually maintenance-free. Highly resistant to staining, frost and fading — ideal for Manchester's climate. Available in stone, concrete and wood-effect finishes. Can be butt-jointed for a seamless look.

Lifespan25+ years
Install2–4 days

Premium Finish

Natural Stone Patios

Indian sandstone, limestone, slate and granite — all bedded on a cement/mortar bed over a compacted sub-base. Each stone is unique, giving a premium, characterful finish that suits both period and contemporary Manchester properties. Requires sealing every 3–5 years to maintain appearance.

Lifespan20–30 years
Install2–4 days

Low Maintenance

Resin Bound Patios

The same smooth, seamless resin bound surface we install on driveways works equally well on patios. Fully permeable — no puddles, no drainage issues. Available in 30+ aggregate colours. Weed-resistant, frost- resistant and practically maintenance-free.

Lifespan15–25 years

Common Questions

Patio FAQ — Manchester

Questions we hear regularly from Manchester homeowners about patio installation.

How much does a patio cost in Manchester?

Patio costs in Manchester depend on surface type and size. Block paving from £50–120/m², resin bound from £40–80/m², natural stone from £60–130/m², and porcelain from £70–150/m². A typical 20–30m² patio costs £1,500–£4,500 fully installed including sub-base, bedding, and waste removal. We provide free written quotes.

Do I need planning permission for a patio in Manchester?

Most patios don't require planning permission as they fall within permitted development rights. Exceptions include properties in conservation areas or listed buildings, or where a raised patio affects drainage significantly. We advise on this at the free site survey — it's rarely an issue for standard ground-level patios.

What's the best patio surface for Manchester gardens?

Given Manchester's rainfall, drainage is the key consideration. Resin bound and permeable block paving handle water without pooling. Porcelain is highly popular for modern properties — easy to clean, frost-resistant, and available in large formats. Natural stone gives a premium timeless finish. We'll recommend what genuinely suits your garden at the free survey.

How long does patio installation take in Manchester?

A typical 20–30m² patio takes 2–4 days depending on surface type and groundworks needed. Resin bound is fastest at 1–3 days. Block paving, natural stone and porcelain typically take 2–4 days. We agree a programme before work starts and keep to it.

Can you install a patio and driveway at the same time?

Yes — and it's usually the most cost-effective approach. Groundworks, sub-base and waste removal are shared across both areas, reducing cost per m². The same crew completes both in one visit. If you're considering updating both, ask us to quote them together.

Do you guarantee patio installations in Manchester?

Yes. Every patio installation across Manchester and Greater Manchester comes with a 10-year workmanship guarantee. We've been trading since 1994 and stand behind every job we complete — if something fails due to workmanship within 10 years, we return and fix it at no charge.
