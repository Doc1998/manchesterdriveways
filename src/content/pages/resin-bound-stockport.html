What Is a Resin Bound Driveway & Why Is It Right for Your Stockport Home?

A resin bound driveway is created by mixing natural aggregate stone with a clear, UV-stable polyurethane resin, then trowelling the mixture onto a prepared base to create a smooth, seamless surface. Because the stones are fully encapsulated within the resin, the result is a hard-wearing, attractive finish with no loose stones.

Crucially for Stockport homeowners, resin bound driveways are fully permeable — rainwater drains through the surface into the ground below rather than running off into drains or onto the road. This means resin bound surfaces comply with Stockport Council's planning rules under Permitted Development, so in most cases no planning permission is required.

Resin Bound vs Resin Bonded: Which Is Right for Stockport?

We're often asked about the difference between resin bound and resin bonded driveways. Resin bonded scatters loose aggregate onto a resin-coated surface — it looks decorative but is not permeable, can lose stones over time, and generally has a shorter lifespan. Resin bound fully encapsulates every stone within the resin mix, creating a solid, permeable surface with no loose material. It's the higher-quality, longer-lasting choice — and the only type we install across Stockport.

Resin Bound Driveways Stockport — FAQs

How much does a resin bound driveway cost in Stockport?

Resin bound driveways in Stockport typically cost between £40 and £80 per square metre, depending on the size, the aggregate chosen, existing surface condition, and any base preparation required. A standard domestic driveway of 40m² would typically cost between £1,600 and £3,200. We provide free, itemised quotes for all properties across Stockport — contact us for an accurate price for your specific project.

Do I need planning permission for a resin driveway in Stockport?

In most cases, no. Resin bound surfaces are fully permeable, meaning rainwater drains through the surface naturally rather than running off into the road. This satisfies Stockport Council's Sustainable Drainage (SuDS) planning requirements, so resin bound driveways generally fall within Permitted Development rights and don't require a planning application. Our team will confirm this applies to your specific property during the free site survey.

How long does a resin driveway installation take in Stockport?

Most resin bound driveway installations across Stockport are completed within a single day. The resin and aggregate are mixed on-site and trowelled onto the prepared base — a rapid process compared to block paving. The surface can be walked on within a few hours of completion, and is ready for vehicles within 24 hours, depending on temperature and conditions.

What is the difference between resin bound and resin bonded?

Resin bound driveways mix the aggregate fully within the resin before laying, fully encapsulating every stone to create a solid, seamless, permeable surface. Resin bonded involves spreading loose aggregate onto a resin-coated base — the stones sit on top rather than within the resin. Resin bonded is not permeable, tends to shed stones over time, and typically has a shorter lifespan. We only install resin bound driveways, as they offer the best long-term results for Stockport homeowners.

How long will my resin driveway last?

A professionally installed resin bound driveway typically lasts between 15 and 25 years. The UV-stable resin won't yellow or crack, and the fully-encapsulated aggregate means there are no loose stones to scatter. The surface is resistant to frost, weeds and oil stains. We back all our Stockport resin driveway installations with a 10-year workmanship guarantee for your complete peace of mind.

Can resin be installed over my existing driveway?

In many cases, yes — resin bound can be installed over an existing solid, stable surface such as tarmac or concrete, which can significantly reduce the cost and installation time. However, if the existing surface is cracked, sunken or unstable, it will need to be broken out and a new base installed first. We'll assess this during your free site visit and give you honest advice on the most cost-effective approach for your Stockport property.
