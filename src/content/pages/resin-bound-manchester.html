Why Manchester Homeowners Choose Resin Bound Driveways

Resin bound driveways have become one of the most popular surfacing choices for Manchester homeowners — and it's easy to see why. A mixture of UV-stable polyurethane resin and natural aggregate is hand-trowelled onto a prepared base to create a smooth, seamless surface that looks premium, performs brilliantly, and asks almost nothing of you in terms of maintenance.

For Manchester's varied housing stock — from the Victorian terraces of Chorlton and Didsbury to the executive homes of Sale and Altrincham — resin bound is incredibly versatile. It suits period properties just as well as modern new builds, and with over 20 aggregate colour blends available, we'll match the surface to your home.

Crucially, resin bound is fully permeable, meaning rainwater drains through the surface naturally. This satisfies Manchester City Council's Sustainable Drainage (SuDS) requirements, so in most cases no planning permission is required — even for large driveways.

  • Fully permeable — usually no planning permission required
  • Completed in a single day for most Manchester properties
  • UV-stable resin — will not yellow, crack or fade
  • Weed-resistant — no joints for weeds to penetrate
  • Slip-resistant finish — ideal for sloped Manchester driveways
  • Low maintenance — a periodic jet wash keeps it pristine
  • 10-year workmanship guarantee on every installation

Common Questions

Resin Driveways Manchester — FAQs

How much do resin driveways cost in Manchester?

Resin bound driveways in Manchester typically cost between £40 and £80 per square metre depending on size, the aggregate chosen, existing surface condition, and any preparation work required. A typical 40m² driveway would cost approximately £1,600–£3,200. We provide free, itemised quotes — contact us for an accurate price for your specific Manchester property.

Do resin driveways need planning permission in Manchester?

In most cases, no. Resin bound is fully permeable, so rainwater drains naturally through the surface rather than running off into drains or the road. This satisfies Manchester City Council's SuDS planning requirements, meaning resin bound driveways generally fall within Permitted Development rights. We'll confirm this applies to your property during the free site visit.

How long does a resin driveway last in Manchester?

A professionally installed resin bound driveway typically lasts 15–25 years. The UV-stable resin resists fading and cracking, and the encapsulated aggregate means no loose stones. The surface is resistant to Manchester's frost and freeze-thaw cycles, and requires only occasional jet washing to maintain. All our Manchester installations are backed by a 10-year workmanship guarantee.

How long does installation take in Manchester?

Most resin bound driveways in Manchester are completed in a single day. The resin and aggregate are mixed on-site and trowelled onto the prepared base — a quick process compared to block paving. The surface is walkable within a few hours of completion and ready for vehicles within 24 hours, depending on temperature and conditions.

Can resin be installed over existing tarmac in Manchester?

Often yes — if the existing tarmac or concrete base is sound and stable, resin bound can be laid directly over it, reducing both cost and installation time significantly. If the existing surface is cracked, sunken or draining poorly, we'll recommend a full base installation for the best long-term result. We'll assess this honestly during your free survey.

What colours are available for resin driveways in Manchester?

We offer over 20 natural and crushed aggregate blends, from warm golden gravels and amber tones to cool slate greys and charcoal finishes. All aggregates use UV-stable resin so they won't fade over time. We bring sample boards to every site visit so you can see colours in context against your property and decide with confidence before committing.
